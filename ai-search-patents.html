Why Patents Matter

Everyone reads the blog posts. Few people read the patents. But patents reveal what companies are actually building—not just what they're saying at conferences. We analyzed 13 recently published patents from Google and OpenAI to understand the technical infrastructure behind AI search.

Google Patents

Privacy Controls for Embeddings

US-20250384079-A1

"Embeddings can be shared with others via digital keys, allowing limited access to search for that person in media collections without exposing the actual embedding data."

Key mechanism: Revocable key-based access to embeddings. Users can share access to their data without exposing the underlying vectors.

Implication: Privacy-first search architecture. Google is building systems where personalization doesn't require exposing user data.

Multi-User LLM Conversations

US-20250384215-A1

"A two-stage ML model approach where a first model evaluates whether user input targets another participant or the assistant, then a second model generates contextual responses only when appropriate."

Key mechanism: AI that knows when to respond in group conversations vs. when to stay silent.

Implication: AI assistants will increasingly participate in group contexts—not just one-on-one chat.

Matryoshka Embeddings (Reduced Dimensionality)

US-20250384346-A1

"Matryoshka embeddings—nested representations where progressively lower-dimensional versions are extracted from a single source. Enabling efficient retrieval at various computational budget levels."

Key mechanism: Variable-quality search based on computational budget. One model produces multiple quality levels (512-, 256-, or 128-dimensional from 1024-dimensional source).

Implication: Faster, cheaper search at scale. Google can serve billions of queries by dynamically choosing embedding quality.

Query Routing from Multiple Sources

US-20250370993-A1

"The system uses a trained model to assess sampling ratios and information loss to determine which source will produce superior accuracy within acceptable latency constraints."

Key mechanism: Dynamic source selection per query, balancing speed vs. accuracy.

Implication: AI search isn't one system—it's many systems, intelligently routed per query.

Open-Vocabulary Object Detection

US-20250384650-A1

"Approximately 200x computational savings versus competing methods. Enables direct transfer between datasets by swapping vocabulary without fine-tuning."

Implication: Multimodal search will scale. Recognizing objects in images becomes 200x cheaper.

In-Context Example Selection

US-20250384666-A1

"Strategically repeats difficult demonstration examples within the context input. Simply adding more demonstration examples doesn't guarantee improved performance."

Implication: Prompt engineering matters. The examples you show AI determine output quality.
OpenAI Patents

Multi-Agent Shared Workspace

US-20250377934-A1

"Agents have access to a common workspace and can view the state of a workspace. Agents autonomously decide whether to 'yield' (defer to others) or 'act' (respond) to workspace updates."

Implication: The future isn't single-agent AI—it's teams of specialized agents working together.

Agent-to-Agent Interactions

US-20250373574-A1

"AI agent instances interacting with each other on behalf of different user accounts. Recipient users can block unwanted agent communications through blocklists."

Described as a "'have your people call my people' workflow."

Implication: AI agents will negotiate and transact on your behalf—with consent frameworks.

Long Running Tasks

US-20250378278-A1

"Users can submit follow-up prompts to modify, clarify, or add to the original request without waiting for completion. Particularly useful for multi-hour or multi-day computational tasks."

Implication: AI won't just answer questions—it will execute multi-day projects you can adjust along the way.

Schema-Based API Integration

US-20250110811-A1

"Publishers can host a manifest file and specification document at their own domains. The system automatically discovers and integrates these APIs, enabling it to intelligently call external endpoints without requiring retraining."

Implication: Your website can become AI-callable. Publish a manifest, and AI agents can use your service.

Selective Learning/Personalization

US-20250200361-A1

"Learn such facts, preferences, or contexts from conversational prompts." Stored in a "personalization notepad" for "generating tailored responses across sessions." "Users can request deletion of learned information."

Implication: AI personalization will be implicit and continuous—but with user control.

Contrastive Embeddings

US-20240370779-A1

"Leverages naturally-occurring pairs from unlabeled data (adjacent text, docstrings paired with code implementations). Initializes with pre-trained generative models before fine-tuning."

Implication: Better search through understanding relationships, not just keywords.

Custom Model Instructions

US-20250384222-A1

"Structured storage of user preferences in session-specific caches. Dynamic integration of instructions into system prompts based on trigger events."

Implication: AI that knows when to apply your preferences and when to ignore them.

The Big Picture

What Google is Building

Efficiency: Matryoshka embeddings, query routing, computational savings. Google is optimizing for billions of queries at scale.

Privacy: Key-based access to embeddings, user-controlled sharing. Personalization without exposure.

Multimodal: Visual search, open-vocabulary detection. 200x cheaper object recognition.

What OpenAI is Building

Agent systems: Multi-agent workspaces, agent-to-agent communication. Teams of AI working together.

Long-running tasks: Async execution, mid-flight modification. AI that works on multi-day projects.

Personalization: Implicit learning, preference storage. AI that remembers you—with your consent.

What This Means for Content Creators

Your content trains these systems. High-quality content becomes training data for better AI.

APIs matter. Consider how AI agents might interact with your services.

Multimodal is coming. Invest in images, video, structured data.

Privacy controls exist. Users will control how their data is used.

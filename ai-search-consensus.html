What Google & Bing Agree On

8 points of consensus on AI search optimization from official sources

1 podcast, 9 blog posts
Publishers: Google, Microsoft

Executive Summary

After analyzing official guidance from Google Search Liaison Danny Sullivan, Google Search Advocate John Mueller, and Microsoft's Bing team, we identified 8 points of complete consensus on AI search optimization. The core message: there's no special trick. Quality content, technical fundamentals, and genuine expertise matter more than ever.

The 8 Consensus Points

1 No Special AI Optimization Required

"There's not a lot you actually really need to be worrying about" regarding new AI optimization tactics.

— Danny Sullivan, Google Search Liaison, Search Off the Record podcast

"The more you're trying to optimize or GEO or whatever you think it is for a specific kind of system, the more you're potentially going to get away from the main goal."

— Danny Sullivan

Takeaway: Stop chasing "AEO" or "GEO" tactics. Focus on creating genuinely helpful content.

2 E-E-A-T Signals Matter More

"E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) remains crucial for AI-driven search."

— Google Search Central Blog, May 2025

"Produce authoritative, well-researched material demonstrating genuine expertise."

— Google Search Central Blog

Takeaway: Authority signals are amplified in AI search. Demonstrate genuine expertise through content quality, not keyword tricks.

3 Structured Data Helps But Isn't Magic

"Structured data could be helpful for AI formats but is not a secret weapon—it wasn't your secret. Aha, structured data now I have it."

— Danny Sullivan

"Implement proper schema markup/structured data to help AI systems understand content."

— Google Search Central Blog

Takeaway: Structured data helps systems understand your content, but it won't compensate for thin or low-quality content.

4 Multimodal Content Creates Opportunities

"If you have been more textual in nature and you haven't been doing images or video, then by making sure you have more content-rich original content, you potentially are going to have opportunities."

— Danny Sullivan

"Multimodal means searching one way and getting information back another (e.g., video query → text response)."

— Danny Sullivan

Takeaway: Diversify beyond text. Images, video, and audio content will have more opportunities in AI search.

5 Conversion Quality > Traffic Quantity

"The path to conversion no longer starts with a click."

— Bing Webmaster Blog, November 2025

"Citations and placement in AI answers are becoming the visibility signals that decide who enters the final click."

— Bing Webmaster Blog

"AI formats put people into a better state of contextual awareness—users understand better what they're getting into."

— Danny Sullivan

"People arriving from AI formats seem to be more engaged when they get there."

— Danny Sullivan

Takeaway: Measure quality of conversions, not just traffic volume. AI visitors have higher intent clarity.

6 Technical Fundamentals Still Matter

"The lastmod field in your sitemap remains a key signal, helping Bing prioritize URLs for recrawling and reindexing."

— Bing Webmaster Blog, July 2025

"If you're using a popular CMS like WordPress or Wix, you don't have to worry about any of those technical details."

— John Mueller, Google

Takeaway: Sitemaps, canonical tags, crawlability—the fundamentals persist. Get them right once, then focus on content.

7 Duplicate Content Hurts AI Visibility

"Duplicate content doesn't trigger search penalties on its own, but reduces visibility by diluting authority, confusing intent, and slowing updates."

— Bing Webmaster Blog, December 2025

"LLMs cluster near-duplicate URLs and select one to represent the set—potentially an outdated version."

— Bing Webmaster Blog

"Less is more. Clean, consolidated signals help search engines understand intent."

— Bing Webmaster Blog

Takeaway: Consolidate similar content. Use canonical tags. Don't create pages for every keyword variation.

8 Citations Are the New Visibility Metric

"Citations and placement in AI answers are becoming the visibility signals that decide who enters the final click."

— Bing Webmaster Blog, November 2025

"Prominently cites sources so users know exactly where the information came from."

— Bing, Copilot Search announcement, April 2025

"Inline links embed citations throughout responses."

— Bing

Takeaway: Being cited in AI answers matters as much as ranking in traditional search. Track citations, not just rankings.

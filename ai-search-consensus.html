What Google & Bing Agree On

We analyzed official statements from Danny Sullivan, John Mueller, and Microsoft's Bing team. Here are the 8 points they completely agree on.

Patrick Gallagher Updated Dec 25, 2025
Independent + Reliable Sources

Key Takeaways

  • No special "AI SEO" needed — Focus on quality content, not new optimization tricks
  • E-E-A-T matters more than ever — Authority signals are amplified in AI results
  • Citations replace rankings — Being cited in AI answers is the new visibility metric
  • Quality beats quantity — AI visitors convert better with higher intent clarity

Executive Summary

After analyzing official guidance from Google Search Liaison Danny Sullivan, Google Search Advocate John Mueller, and Microsoft's Bing team, we identified 8 points of complete consensus on AI search optimization.

The core message is surprisingly simple: there's no special trick. Quality content, technical fundamentals, and genuine expertise matter more than ever. Here's what both search giants agree you should focus on.

The 8 Consensus Points

1

No Special AI Optimization Required

"There's not a lot you actually really need to be worrying about regarding new AI optimization tactics."

— Danny Sullivan, Google Search Liaison
Stop chasing "AEO" or "GEO" tactics. Focus on creating genuinely helpful content.
2

E-E-A-T Signals Matter More

"E-E-A-T remains crucial for AI-driven search. Produce authoritative, well-researched material demonstrating genuine expertise."

— Google Search Central Blog
Authority signals are amplified in AI search. Demonstrate genuine expertise through content quality.
3

Structured Data Helps But Isn't Magic

"Structured data could be helpful for AI formats but is not a secret weapon."

— Danny Sullivan
Schema helps systems understand content, but won't compensate for thin or low-quality content.
4

Multimodal Content Creates Opportunities

"If you haven't been doing images or video, you potentially are going to have opportunities."

— Danny Sullivan
Diversify beyond text. Images, video, and audio content will have more opportunities in AI search.
5

Conversion Quality > Traffic Quantity

"The path to conversion no longer starts with a click. Citations and placement in AI answers are becoming the visibility signals that decide who enters the final click."

— Bing Webmaster Blog, November 2025

"AI formats put people into a better state of contextual awareness—users understand better what they're getting into. People arriving from AI formats seem to be more engaged."

— Danny Sullivan
Measure quality of conversions, not just traffic volume. AI visitors have higher intent clarity.
6

Technical Fundamentals Still Matter

"The lastmod field in your sitemap remains a key signal, helping Bing prioritize URLs for recrawling."

— Bing Webmaster Blog
Sitemaps, canonical tags, crawlability—the fundamentals persist. Get them right once.
7

Duplicate Content Hurts AI Visibility

"LLMs cluster near-duplicate URLs and select one to represent the set—potentially an outdated version."

— Bing Webmaster Blog
Consolidate similar content. Use canonical tags. Don't create pages for every keyword variation.
8

Citations Are the New Visibility Metric

"Citations and placement in AI answers are becoming the visibility signals that decide who enters the final click. Inline links embed citations throughout responses."

— Bing Webmaster Blog & Copilot Search announcement
Being cited in AI answers matters as much as ranking in traditional search. Track citations, not just rankings.
